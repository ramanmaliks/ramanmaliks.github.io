There are several types of Fibonacci tools commonly used in technical analysis. Here are the main ones: 

 

1. Fibonacci Retracement: Fibonacci retracement is perhaps the most widely used Fibonacci tool. It involves identifying a significant price move (swing high to swing low in a downtrend, or swing low to swing high in an uptrend) and then plotting retracement levels based on Fibonacci ratios (typically 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, and sometimes 78.6%). These levels are used to identify potential areas of support or resistance where the price may pause or reverse. 

 

2. Fibonacci Extensions: Fibonacci extensions are used to identify potential price targets beyond the initial trend. They are based on Fibonacci ratios applied to an initial price move (usually from a swing low to a swing high in an uptrend, or from a swing high to a swing low in a downtrend). Common extension levels include 127.2%, 161.8%, and 261.8%, among others. Traders use these extension levels to identify where a trend might encounter resistance or where a trend might accelerate. 

 

3. Fibonacci Fans: Fibonacci fans consist of diagonal lines drawn from a significant low or high to other points on the chart, based on Fibonacci ratios. The resulting fan-shaped lines can help traders identify potential support and resistance levels as well as trendlines. The most commonly used ratios for Fibonacci fans are 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8%. 

 

4. Fibonacci Arcs: Fibonacci arcs are curves drawn between two points on a price chart, usually a swing low and swing high, based on Fibonacci ratios. These arcs are used to identify potential areas of support and resistance as well as trendlines. The most commonly used arcs are based on the 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% Fibonacci ratios. 

 

5. Fibonacci Time Zones: Fibonacci time zones are vertical lines drawn on a price chart based on Fibonacci ratios, typically applied to significant highs or lows. These lines are used to identify potential reversal points or significant turning points in the market based on time. The most commonly used time zones are based on the 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% Fibonacci ratios. 

 

Each type of Fibonacci tool has its own strengths and weaknesses, and traders often use a combination of these tools to analyze price movements and make trading decisions. It's important to understand how each tool works and to practice using them in different market conditions to become proficient at applying Fibonacci analysis effectively.
